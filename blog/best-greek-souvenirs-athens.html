Best Greek Souvenirs in Athens: Shopping Guide

February 25, 2026

A practical Athens souvenir guide with authentic Greek brands, local food products, fashion, beauty, and where to shop in central Athens.

Looking for meaningful souvenirs from Greece, not random tourist items? Athens is full of beautiful, edible, and wearable treasures made by real Greek brands and artisans.

Where can I buy authentic Greek spices and herbs?

One of the most aromatic streets in Athens is Evripidou Street in the city center.

This street is famous for:

  • Dried oregano from Greek mountains
  • Thyme and sage
  • Greek mountain tea
  • Cinnamon, cloves, and spice blends
  • Saffron from Kozani

Walking here feels like stepping into an old Mediterranean market. The scent alone is worth the visit.

What to buy:

  • Fresh dried oregano (light and fragrant)
  • Greek mountain tea bags (easy gift)
  • Mixed spice blends for souvlaki or Greek salads

These are lightweight, affordable, and very Greek.

Where can I find beautiful Greek art and memorabilia?

For high-quality Greek-themed art and design objects, visit Amorgos - Arts and Crafts on Kodrou Street in Plaka.

This store offers:

  • Greek-made ceramics
  • Modern art prints inspired by ancient Greece
  • Decorative evil eye charms
  • Beautiful home objects with Cycladic aesthetics

It is ideal if you want something tasteful and artistic rather than mass-produced souvenirs.

Where can I buy Greek fashion and couture?

Athens has strong contemporary Greek designers.

Two excellent options:

Nomad

Located on Adrianou Street in Plaka, Nomad offers:

  • Linen dresses
  • Resort wear
  • Earth-tone Mediterranean designs

Minimal, elegant, and very wearable.

Ioanna Kourbela

Ioanna Kourbela is a respected Greek designer known for:

  • High-quality knitwear
  • Timeless silhouettes
  • Neutral colors

Her boutique on Adrianou Street is a strong option if you want a Greek designer piece that lasts for years.

Where can I buy authentic Greek coffee?

For traditional Greek coffee, go to Mokka on Athinas Street.

Mokka roasts Greek coffee the traditional way using sand heating.

What to buy:

  • Ground Greek coffee for briki preparation
  • Small traditional coffee cups
  • Freshly roasted blends

It is one of the most authentic coffee experiences in central Athens.

Where can I buy honey, tahini, halva, and Greek sweets?

One popular food stop in central Athens is Kolios.

At Kolios you will find:

  • Greek thyme honey
  • Tahini in different varieties
  • Traditional halva
  • Nuts and dried fruits
  • Sesame pasteli

These products are perfect edible souvenirs.

For halva, also look for brands like:

  • Haitoglou Bros
  • Olympos

What Greek chocolate brands are worth buying?

Some of the most loved Greek chocolates are:

  • ION
  • Lacta
  • Caprice
  • Chocofreta

You can find them in any supermarket. They are inexpensive and nostalgic for Greeks.

Where can I buy leather sandals and bags?

For real Athenian leather craftsmanship, walk around:

  • Pandrossou Street
  • Monastiraki area

One historic name is Melissinos Art, known for handmade leather sandals inspired by ancient Greek designs.

Also look for modern Greek leather brands such as Ancient Greek Sandals.

What about natural Greek beauty brands?

Greece produces excellent skincare using olive oil and herbs.

Top choices:

  • Korres
  • Apivita
  • Fresh Line

These are widely available in pharmacies and flagship stores.

What are some modern Greek lifestyle brands?

For stylish Greek-made products, check:

  • COCO-MAT for natural home goods
  • Ergon Foods for premium edible gifts
  • Larocheposay for dermatologist‑trusted skincare essentials
  • Dirty Laundry for contemporary Greek fashion with an urban edge

What is the most symbolic Greek souvenir?

The evil eye charm, known as the Mati.

You will find:

  • Blue eye earrings
  • Bracelets
  • Decorative wall charms

It symbolizes protection and good luck and is one of the most recognizable Greek symbols.

Final tips for souvenir shopping in Athens

  • Explore central streets like Athinas, Evripidou, Adrianou, and Kodrou
  • Choose locally produced goods over generic imports
  • Food items like honey, oregano, and chocolate are safe and easy gifts
  • Designer pieces from Greek brands make meaningful, long-lasting souvenirs
