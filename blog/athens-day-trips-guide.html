Best Day Trip Ideas from Athens

January 20, 2025

Discover the best day trips from Athens: Ancient temples, island escapes, thermal spas, and seaside villages. Complete guide with travel times, what to expect, and how to choose.

Athens is extraordinary, but the real magic of Greece isn't contained within the city limits. Just an hour or two away from central Athens‚Äîsometimes less‚Äîyou'll find ancient temples overlooking the Aegean, island towns where time moves differently, thermal spas where Greeks go to decompress, and fishing villages where your biggest decision is which taverna to choose for lunch. These aren't resort destinations or overly polished tourist traps. They're the kind of places Athenians actually escape to on weekends.

Cape Sounion

Best for sunset views and dramatic landscapes

Travel time: About 1 to 1.5 hours by car from central Athens

What to expect:

Cape Sounion is home to the Temple of Poseidon, standing high above the Aegean Sea. The drive itself is part of the experience, following the coastal road through the Athens Riviera with constant sea views.

Once there, you can walk around the archaeological site, admire the massive marble columns, and look out over the cliffs where sea meets sky. The highlight for most visitors is sunset, when the light turns golden and the atmosphere becomes almost cinematic.

What kind of experience it is:

Calm, scenic, and cultural. Ideal for couples, photographers, and anyone who wants a memorable half-day outside the city.

üí° Local tip: Arrive 1.5 hours before sunset to explore the site and secure a good viewing spot. The last entry is usually 30 minutes before closing.

Aegina Island

Best for island vibes without long travel

Travel time: 40 to 75 minutes by ferry from Piraeus, depending on the boat

What to expect:

Aegina feels like a proper Greek island, even though it's very close to Athens. You can stroll around the port town, swim at nearby beaches, or visit the Temple of Aphaia, one of the best-preserved ancient temples in Greece.

The island is also famous for pistachios, so expect pistachio ice cream, sweets, and snacks everywhere. Lunch by the sea is easy and relaxed, with tavernas lining the harbor.

What kind of experience it is:

A classic island day, slow-paced and refreshing. Perfect if you want ferries, beaches, and seafood without committing to an overnight stay.

üí° Local tip: Take the fast ferry (Flying Cat or similar) for a 40-minute trip. Book ferry tickets at Ferryhopper to compare schedules and prices.

Chalkida (Evia Island)

Best for something different and local

Travel time: About 1 hour by car or train

What to expect:

Chalkida is connected to the mainland by a bridge and is known for the strange tidal phenomenon in the Euripus Strait, where the water changes direction several times a day.

The town itself is lively and very Greek, with caf√©s, seafood tavernas, and a long seaside promenade. It's less touristy than other destinations, which makes it feel authentic and relaxed.

What kind of experience it is:

Local, easygoing, and food-focused. Great if you want to see how Athenians do weekend escapes.

üí° Local tip: Ask locals about the tide-changing times (the phenomenon happens roughly every 6 hours). Watching the water reverse direction is genuinely fascinating.

Loutraki

Best for spa vibes and seaside walks

Travel time: About 1 to 1.5 hours by car or bus

What to expect:

Loutraki is known for its natural mineral springs and long beachfront. You can enjoy a swim, walk along the promenade, or visit a spa for thermal treatments.

The town has a classic resort feel, with caf√©s facing the sea and easy access to nearby attractions like Lake Vouliagmeni (the Corinthian one) or the Corinth Canal.

What kind of experience it is:

Relaxing and low-effort. Ideal if you want a wellness-style day with minimal planning.

üí° Local tip: Combine Loutraki with a quick stop at the Corinth Canal (15 minutes away) for dramatic photos of the narrow waterway carved through solid rock.

Best for a quick beach escape close to Athens

Travel time: About 45 minutes by car

What to expect:

Porto Rafti is a coastal town popular with locals, especially in summer. It has organized beaches, small coves, and plenty of seaside tavernas serving fresh fish and traditional Greek dishes.

There are no major attractions or museums here‚Äîthe appeal is purely the sea, food, and relaxed atmosphere.

What kind of experience it is:

Simple and summery. Perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and lunch by the water.

üí° Local tip: Visit on a weekday to avoid weekend crowds. The beach bars and tavernas are quieter and more authentic during the week.

Best for combining history and the sea

Travel time: 45 minutes to 1 hour by car

What to expect:

This area blends historical importance with coastal life. You can visit the site of the Battle of Marathon and the Marathon Tomb, then head to the nearby beaches of Zouberi or Nea Makri for swimming and food.

Nea Makri in particular has a long seaside road full of tavernas and caf√©s, making it easy to end the day with a relaxed meal by the sea.

What kind of experience it is:

Balanced and flexible. A good mix of culture, beach time, and local dining.

üí° Local tip: The Archaeological Museum of Marathon is small but excellent. Spend 30-45 minutes there before heading to the beach.
